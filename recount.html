My One-Year Journey Learning to Code

I am a 10th-grade student majoring in Software Engineering (RPL). When I first started two semesters ago, I knew nothing about technology. I didn't know how to build a website or what coding was. I was just a regular internet user.

In the first semester, everything was confusing. I started learning the basics, like HTML and CSS. At first, looking at all the code and symbols made me dizzy. But slowly, I began to understand how to create a simple web page and make it look good with colors.

In the second semester, it got harder. We started learning about frameworks and more difficult programming logic. I felt really happy when my code worked and I could see the result on my browser. I went from knowing nothing to building my own simple website.

However, I have to be honest: learning this takes time. Even after two semesters, I am still not an expert. I still often look at tutorials, search for solutions on the internet when I find errors, and feel confused when things get complicated.

My experience this year taught me that learning to code is a marathon, not a sprint. I am proud that I went from zero to knowing the basics. I know I still have a lot to learn, and I will keep practicing to get better at building websites in the future.
